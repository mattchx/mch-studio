Project Snapshot

This project turned a manual client intake process into an automated online form that emails completed results to the team.

The business impact was straightforward: reduce manual copying, cut error risk, and save team time every time the form is completed.

A dashboard can still be added later, but the high-impact part was the workflow itself: send the link, collect the details, run the calculations, and get the results to the team automatically.

The value was not just digitizing a form. The value was saving real team time inside a repeated sales and intake process.

The Problem

The existing process depended on several manual steps. A client or prospect would fill out a Client Profile Questionnaire, send it back, and then a team member would review the response and manually transfer the details into a spreadsheet or calculation tool.

That process can work at low volume, but it creates extra manual work. It asks more of the client, takes time from the team, and creates more chances for mistakes that can slow down the next step.

A copied number can land in the wrong place. A required field can be missed. A calculation can be delayed while someone checks formatting or reconciles unclear information. Even when the team is careful, repeated manual transfer costs time and increases risk.

The Solution

The solution was an online intake form that automated the most repetitive parts of the process.

The client completes the intake online. The form captures the required answers in a consistent format, runs the calculations needed for the next step, prepares the result, and emails the completed form to the team.

This keeps the project focused on the work that saves time immediately: collect the right information once, reduce manual copying, and get usable results to the team faster.

Business Impact

1. Time Savings

If a team member previously spent 30 to 45 minutes reviewing an intake form, copying details into a spreadsheet, checking formatting, and preparing calculations, this workflow can save a meaningful amount of time each time the form is completed. That time has real cost, especially when the same process repeats across multiple opportunities.

2. Reduced Human Error

Manual transfer creates risk. A consistent form and automatic calculations reduce the number of places where information can be copied incorrectly, missed, or interpreted differently from person to person.

3. Improved Client Experience

Clients no longer need to manage a document, format answers, attach a file, and send it back manually. They receive a link, complete the form, and submit. That makes the process feel clearer, more modern, and easier to complete.

4. More Capacity For Higher-Value Work

The time saved can be redirected toward reviewing the actual opportunity, responding faster, preparing recommendations, supporting clients, and moving qualified work forward.

5. Revenue Potential Without Overclaiming

This version does not claim a measured revenue increase. But it does create conditions that can support revenue: less friction for clients, faster team response, more consistent information, and more team capacity for sales and service work.

Careful Claim

The strongest honest claim is not "this automatically closes more deals." It is "this gives the team a faster, cleaner, more consistent process that can make it easier to close and support more work."

What Comes Next

The online form is the high-impact layer because it removes manual copying and sends usable results to the team.

A dashboard can be added later if the team needs saved forms, assignment, status tracking, reporting, admin views, CRM integration, or analytics.

The important part is that the foundation already improves the workflow. It saves time, reduces error risk, and creates a cleaner experience before any heavier software layer is added.

Takeaway

The point was not simply to build a better form. The point was to make a repeated business workflow faster, cleaner, and less dependent on manual copying.

By turning the client intake form into an online form with automatic calculations and email delivery, the business gets a cleaner client experience, a more consistent team review process, and more team capacity to spend on higher-value work.

That is the practical value of this kind of build: not software for its own sake, but an automated workflow that can save real time and money while giving the business a stronger system to grow from.
