# MCH site redesign series

Purpose: turn the MCH Projects site rebuild into a 30-day content lane built around real screenshots, short teardown notes, and practical talking-head or screen-recording posts.

## Position

Your website should feel owned, not rented.

The series should show the site being rebuilt as proof of the MCH Projects offer: owned code, working lead capture, plain-English tracking, practical content, and marketing systems a service business can keep.

## Content Pillars

- Screenshot proof: show the actual site, pages, forms, tracking, and assets.
- Rebuild decisions: explain why a section changed, what got removed, and what the new version is supposed to do.
- Owner pain: name the agency/site problems that make service businesses skeptical.
- Practical walkthroughs: short talking-head or Loom/OBS-style clips explaining one decision at a time.
- Offer clarity: connect the rebuild back to the service without making every post a pitch.

## 30-Day Shape

Week 1: Ownership
- Day 1: Built to be owned. Current bundle: `mch-projects-biz-intro`.
- Day 2: Why a website should not feel rented from an agency.
- Day 3: What a service business should own: domain, code, forms, analytics, ads accounts.
- Day 4: Screenshot teardown of the homepage hero.
- Day 5: Talking-head: the simplest way to spot a site you do not really control.

Week 2: Lead Capture
- Show the form flow.
- Explain what happens after a lead submits.
- Compare "contact form as decoration" vs "contact form as system."
- Screenshot a plain-English lead handoff.
- Talk through the follow-up gap most sites leave open.

Week 3: Proof and Tracking
- Show what plain-English reporting should look like.
- Explain which numbers matter before ad spend.
- Show a before/after of messy vs useful tracking.
- Use one short screen recording to walk through a lead path.
- Turn a common agency report complaint into a practical fix.

Week 4: Content and Offers
- Show how a site creates post ideas.
- Reuse one site section as a LinkedIn post.
- Reuse one FAQ as an ad angle.
- Show a simple offer page or service section teardown.
- Close with the MCH Projects offer: a site and marketing system the owner can keep.

## Asset Rule

Use real screenshots first. Use generated visuals only when they add context that a screenshot cannot show.
